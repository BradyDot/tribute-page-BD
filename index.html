JJR Tolkien

The father of modern fantasy

J.R.R. Tolkien is responsible for some of the most famous and best regarded fantasy books of all time including The Hobbit and The Lord of The Rings.

  • 1892John Ronald Reuel Tolkien is born.
  • 1892-1903Tolkien’s father died when he was young, and his mother died when he was 12. She did however, ensure that he was raised Catholic, as he later thanked her for in his letters. He lived in a variety of places, being taken care of by his guardian, Father Morgan.
  • 1905-1911Tolkien got his education, eventually attending and graduating from Exter College studying English language and literature.
  • 1916Tolkien married Edith Mary Bratt, a woman he fell in love with during his years of schooling.
  • 1916-1920Tolkien served in the military, serving on the home front and in several battles, including the Battle of the Somme. He retired from military service as a lieutenant.
  • 1918-1959Tolkien had a prolific carrear as an academic and worked on several linguistic projects.
  • 1930'sTolkien wrote The Hobbit, which began as a bedtime story for his children, but would then kick start his career as a fantasy author.
  • 1937-1949Tolkien wrote the Lord of the Rings series, with all books published between 1954 and 1955. This series would shape fantasy writing forever.
  • 1959Tolkien and Edith retire to Bournemouth. He continues to receive more fame and money and begins to live life as a famous author in upper-middle class society.
  • 1973J.R.R. Tolkien died at the age of 81, two years after his wife, Edith. He was buried next to his wife, with inscriptions on their tombstones referencing characters from his books inspired by the two of them.

“[The purpose of life is] to increase according to our capacity our knowledge of God by all the means we have, and to be moved by it to praise and thanks”. -J.R.R. Tolkien
